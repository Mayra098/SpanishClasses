Spanish Language Levels

This level is aimed towards students with no prior knowledge of Spanish. You will start to become familiar with the language, learning to use familiar everyday expressions and basic phrases.

At this level, you will expand your vocabulary and be able to express yourself in various communicative contexts using the present and past tenses. You will be able to engage in basic conversations on familiar topics and understand simple texts.

At the end of B1, you will be able to interact in a great variety of situations: exposing a problem, expressing your opinion, asking for explanations, talking about the past and explaining future events. You will be able to understand the main ideas of clear and coherent texts about topics you are familiar with.

After finishing the B2 course, you will be able to explain and defend your opinions during a debate, to expose the advantages and disadvantages of an issue, to speculate on possible situations, and to communicate with native speakers with more fluency and spontaneity.

At the end of level C1, you will have a linguistic repertoire that will allow you to have a fluent, wide and precise communication. You will be able to adapt yourself to different registers, and thus to communicate in different social environments.

At this level, you will be able to understand almost everything you read or hear. You will be able to express yourself spontaneously, with a great fluency and precision, which will allow you to identify and reproduce subtle nuances of meaning.

Methodology

Our approach to teaching is to focus on the integration of skills in context, meaning we not only take into account theory and grammar, but oral and written interactions as well, in addition to reading and listening comprehension. A big proportion of class time is devoted to genuine communication.

Students are encouraged to be creative with their use of the language and active in their own learning process. Students are given advice on how to continue their learning at home and when they return to their own country. Homework is set regularly, checked by the teacher and remedial work is given if necessary.

Students are valued for the knowledge and experience they bring, and are encouraged to share stories about their lives with each other.
